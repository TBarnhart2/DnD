## Overview
This session will establish the new party composition and the dire consequences of the previous session's failure. The adventurers will awaken in a small sanctuary, be confronted by a furious Tua, and receive a critical, high-stakes mission. The goal is not to fix their previous mistake, but to contain the damage by journeying into the dangerous rift they helped create.

### **Part 1: The Sunborn Sanctuary**

- **Location Description: The Infirmary**
    
``` 
You awaken not to the smell of dust and ozone, but to the scent of strange, sweet herbs. You lie on a simple but comfortable cot in a room carved from glowing, golden stone. Soft light emanates from the walls themselves, casting no shadows. Other beds hold a few other individuals, all tended to by robed figures moving with quiet purpose. Through an archway, you can see a tranquil garden of luminescent plants. The air is warm and still, a stark contrast to the violent world you left behind. This is a pocket of calm in the heart of the cataclysm.
```

    
- **Setup:** Alora and the three new player characters all awaken in the infirmary of the Sunborn Sanctuary. They are wounded, having been rescued from the edge of the new rift by Sunborn scouts.
    
- **Key NPC: Lyra Solari (Sunborn Elder)**
    ![[Pasted image 20250609112359.png]]
    - **Goal:** To gently orient the party.
        - Inform them they are in a hidden Sunborn Sanctuary, a haven for a few hundred survivors.
        - Explain that they were found unconscious and brought here to be healed.
        - Express sorrow for their losses and the state of the world. Her demeanor is calm, wise, and weary.

### **Part 2: The Confrontation**

- **Setup:** Once the party is well enough to move, Lyra Solari leads them from the infirmary into the central garden chamber of the sanctuary. Shortly after they arrive, Tua appears.
- **Key NPC: Tua (Whisperwind Commander)**
    - **Goal:** To confront the party and impress upon them the severity of their failure before issuing a new order.
        - **Convey Fury:** She should be visibly and verbally furious. Her anger is sharp, fast, and cutting, not brutish.
        - **Assign Blame:** Directly state that the failure at the Deepdweller Nexus caused the deaths of their three former comrades and created the massive, world-altering rift.
            
        - **Explain the Consequences:** The failure did more than just prevent stabilization; it created a **"Void-Tainted Nexus"** that is now actively poisoning the entire Aetheric network and empowering their enemies.
        - **Establish Their Status:** Make it clear that they are not heroes or trusted agents. They are the only assets available, a last resort being used out of sheer desperation. Heimerdinger was right to discard them, and now she's forced to use them.

### **Part 3: The New Directive**

- **Setup:** The initial confrontation with Tua in the Sunborn Sanctuary proceeds, but the mission she gives is different.
- **Tua's Objective:**
    - **Change of Plans:** He will state that Heimerdinger has determined a direct assault on the corrupted Nexus is impossible; the Void corruption is too concentrated. They would be torn apart before they got close.
    - **The Strategic Target:** Heimerdinger has identified a new solution. They must travel to the **Aegis Peak Observatory**, an ancient and abandoned Skyward facility that predates the Aetheric War. This site houses the **Celestial Orrery**, a device that doesn't just map the Aetheric network—it regulates the flow of energy between the Nexus points.
    - **The New Goal:** The party must use the Orrery to manually re-route the flow of Aether, creating a "dam" that isolates the corrupted Deepdweller Nexus from the rest of the network.
    - **The Sunstone Seed's New Purpose:** The seed is no longer for planting. It is now a **Focusing Key**, required to unlock and power the Orrery's main control console.

#### Stage 1: The Ascent
    
 - **Primary Objective:**
    - Successfully climb the treacherous Aegis Peak to reach the entrance of the ancient observatory.
    
- **Challenge Mechanic:**
    - This is a group skill challenge. The party must achieve **3 successes before they accrue 3 failures**.
    - Each party member describes their action and makes one ability check to contribute. A player cannot use the same skill twice in a row.
    
- **Applicable Checks (DC 15):**
    - **Strength (Athletics):** To climb a sheer surface or power through a major obstacle.
    - **Dexterity (Acrobatics):** To leap a chasm or keep balance on a narrow, unstable path.
    - **Wisdom (Survival):** To find the safest route and avoid natural hazards.
    - **Intelligence (Nature):** To understand and predict the mountain's unnatural weather.
    - _DM may allow other creative skill or tool checks._

- **Consequences:**
    - **Individual Check Failure:** The character who failed the check must succeed on a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** or gain one level of exhaustion.
    - **Overall Success:** The party reaches the observatory entrance, tired but unharmed.
    - **Overall Failure:** The party triggers an avalanche. Each member takes **4d8 bludgeoning damage** (DC 14 Dexterity save for half) and gains one level of exhaustion before arriving at the observatory entrance.

**Primary Objective:**

- Find the three **Skyward Keystones** needed to activate the Celestial Orrery. One keystone is located in each of the three main wings of the observatory.

- Each wing is guarded by **two Arcane Sentinels**.
- The Sentinels activate when the party enters a wing or interacts with the main objective within it.

**1. The Living Quarters**

- **Objective:** Retrieve the first Skyward Keystone.
- **Location:** The keystone is locked inside a heavy, reinforced chest in what appears to be the head astronomer's personal chambers.
- **Challenge:**
    - Opening the chest requires a successful **DC 17 Dexterity check** using Thieves' Tools.
    - Alternatively, the chest can be broken open with a successful **DC 20 Strength (Athletics) check**.

**2. The Library**

- **Objectives:** Retrieve the second Skyward Keystone and find the **Orrery Puzzle Handout**.
- **Location:**
    - The keystone is being used as a decorative bookend on a prominent shelf.
    - The puzzle handout (the star chart) is on a large, central lectern.
- **Challenge:**
    - The library is vast. A successful **DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check** is required to sift through the clutter and confirm the star chart on the lectern is the correct document.
```
Log Entry #734,

The dissonance from the Deepdweller Nexus project grows daily. I fear their ambition oversteps their wisdom. Should the worst occur, and the network require a hard restart, the attunement sequence must be precise to prevent total collapse. The memory of creation must be the guide.

All life begins with the **First Light** of the great star, which warms and gives form to the foundational **Stone of the world**.

Only upon a stable foundation can the **Whispering Winds** first stir, carrying the breath of life across the new lands.

The **Silent Moon** must then rise to take its place, a watchful eye to govern the unseen tides of magic and mystery.

And only when the world is held in this careful balance can it be truly anchored to the cosmos, its fate written in the **Distant Stars**.

May this entry never be needed.
```

**3. The Scrying Chamber**

- **Objective:** Retrieve the third Skyward Keystone.
- **Location:** The chamber is dominated by a large, inactive scrying pool and an array of crystal lenses aimed at it. The keystone is set inside the central, largest lens.
- **Challenge:**
    - Removing the keystone requires a delicate touch. A successful **DC 16 Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check** is needed to pry it loose without cracking the lens.
    - **Failure:** On a failed check, the lens cracks, and a pulse of psychic energy hits the character. They must succeed on a **DC 14 Intelligence saving throw** or take **3d6 psychic damage**.

#### Stage 3: The Celestial Orrery

- **Primary Objective:**
    
    - Use the Celestial Orrery to correctly align the Aetheric network and sever the corrupted Deepdweller Nexus, isolating it from the other Nexus points.

- **Challenge Mechanic: The Orrery Puzzle**
    1. **Activation:** The party must place the 3 Skyward Keystones and the Sunstone Seed into the main console to power on the Orrery.
    2. **Alignment:** The party uses the information from the star chart handout found in the library to solve the puzzle. They must align the five rings (Sunborn, Whisperwind, Stonebinder, Moonwhisperer, Skyward) with their corresponding celestial glyphs.
    3. **The Check:** A player uses their action at the console to make a **DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana) check** to align one ring.
    4. **Puzzle Consequence:**
        - **On Success:** The ring locks into place. This triggers the observatory's defenses, animating **one Arcane Sentinel** which attacks the party.
        - **On Failure (by 5 or more):** The console sparks, dealing **3d6 lightning damage** to the operator.
    5. **Final Action:** Once all five rings are aligned, a large lever emerges from the console. Pulling this lever initiates the severance.

- **Climactic Encounter:**
    - Pulling the final lever shatters the Deepdweller "planet" on the Orrery, releasing the Void energy within.
    - This energy coalesces in the center of the room, forming the boss: the **Void-Bound Anomaly**.

- **Consequences:**
    - **Success:** Defeating the Anomaly completes the severance. The Aetheric network is stabilized, and the party's compass realigns to a new, stable Nexus point.
    - **Failure:** If the party is defeated, the Anomaly is left untethered. The Orrery explodes, destroying the observatory and shattering the party's compass. The Aetheric network descends into further chaos.

###### Boss Stats: Void-Bound Anomaly
![[Pasted image 20250609113340.png]]
- **Type:** Medium Aberration
- **Armor Class:** 16
- **Hit Points:** 82 (11d8 + 33)
- **Speed:** 30 ft., fly 30 ft. (hover)
- **Saving Throws:** INT +6, WIS +5
- **Damage Resistances:** Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from non-magical attacks.
- **Condition Immunities:** Prone
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 12
- **Abilities:**
    - **Warp Field (Aura):** The area within 15 feet of the Anomaly is difficult terrain. Any creature that starts its turn in the aura must succeed on a **DC 14 Wisdom saving throw** or have its speed halved until the start of its next turn.
- **Actions:**
    - **Multiattack:** The Anomaly makes two Chaotic Bolt attacks.
    - **Chaotic Bolt:** Ranged Spell Attack: +6 to hit, range 120 ft., one target.
    - **Hit:** 13 (3d8) damage. The damage type is random for each hit (roll a d6): 1-Acid, 2-Cold, 3-Fire, 4-Lightning, 5-Psychic, 6-Force.
    - **Unravel (Recharge 5-6):** The Anomaly targets one creature it can see within 60 feet. The target must succeed on a **DC 14 Charisma saving throw** or be teleported up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space of the Anomaly's choice and be stunned until the end of its next turn.

##### Success vs. Failure

- **On Success:** The Anomaly is destroyed. The Orrery settles into its new, stable configuration without the Deepdweller Nexus. The flow of Aether is successfully re-routed. The party's compass stops spinning and points to a new, stable Nexus point. The immediate threat of spreading corruption is contained. **The party levels up to 7.**
    
- **On Failure (The party is defeated by the Anomaly):** The unstable Void energy, without a host, detonates. The entire Orrery is destroyed in a cataclysmic explosion. The party is blasted from the mountaintop, taking significant damage and waking up miles away. The Aetheric network is now completely unregulated and spirals into chaos. The compass shatters. The world's decline accelerates dramatically, and the party has lost its most valuable strategic tool.
