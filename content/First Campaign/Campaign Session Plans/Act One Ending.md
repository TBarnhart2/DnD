# Act 1 Finale: Aftermath of the Lens Activation

### The Activation

1. As Razor brings the three lens pieces together, they lock into place with an unnatural click
2. The assembled lens begins to pulse with dark energy, growing brighter and more intense
3. The lens emits a blinding flash, followed by a dome of purple-black energy that expands outward
4. The party members feel intense pain and collapse to the ground as darkness overtakes them

### Waking in a Charred World

The scene transitions to:

The party awakens amid ash and embers. The once-magnificent government building is now a skeletal structure of blackened beams and crumbling stone. Daylight filters weakly through a haze of smoke that hangs over everything.

Around them lie the bodies of non-Aetherborns, reduced to withered husks. The few survivors stumble through the ruins with vacant expressions, their skin marked with strange new patterns where the Aetheric energy touched them.

Alora and Draven notice their own Aetheric markings have grown more pronounced, pulsing faintly with power. Mira examines her hands, surprised to find similar markings beginning to appear on her skin.

```
"Consciousness returns slowly, painfully. Your eyes sting as they open to a world transformed. Where once stood the grand government headquarters, now only charred ruins remain. The air is thick with ash that falls like gray snow, coating everything in a film of dust.

The once-polished marble floors are cracked and blackened. Support beams have collapsed, and what was once ceiling is now open to a hazy sky, the sun a dim orange disk struggling to penetrate the smoke.

Around you lie the remains of those who couldn't withstand the pulse from the lens—withered forms that hardly resemble the people they once were. In the distance, you hear the occasional crash as weakened structures continue to give way. The world smells of char and something else—something acrid and unnatural.

You notice your own bodies have changed. Alora and Draven, you now have pronounced Aetheric markings, glowing faintly with new intensity. Mira, as you look at your hands, you see delicate patterns beginning to form on your skin, tracing lines that pulse with a soft light.

As you struggle to your feet, your heads swimming with the effort, your ears ringing with distant screams and the memory of the explosion, you try to make sense of what has happened to the world around you."
```

### The Hook: A Shadowed Observer

As the party takes in the devastation around them, movement catches their eye atop a partially collapsed wall. A figure in a tattered White Lotus robe stands watching them. It's Tua, badly injured but alive.

When he sees they've noticed him, he tosses down a small object that lands at their feet. It's a compass-like device with Aetheric markings that spin and settle pointing northeast. Around its edge is inscribed: "Find the Wellspring. It's our only hope now."

Before they can call out to him, Tua turns away. As he does, they glimpse someone else moving in the shadows behind him—Korruk, his form now partially translucent like living shadow. He locks eyes briefly with the party before both figures disappear behind the ruins.

The compass continues to pulse with Aetheric energy, the only bright thing in this new, charred world.

```
"As you survey the devastation, movement catches your eye from above. Atop a partially collapsed wall about thirty feet away stands a familiar figure in tattered White Lotus robes—Tua. His face is streaked with blood, one arm held protectively against his side. He's watching you intently.

When your eyes meet his, he gives a solemn nod of recognition. Youve never seen his expression before - cold, somber, and scared. Without a word, he reaches into his robe and tosses something that arcs through the ash-filled air, landing with a metallic clink at your feet.

It's a device resembling a compass, though its needle doesn't point north. Strange Aetheric symbols adorn its face, glowing with a soft orange light—the only vibrant color in this gray wasteland. The needle spins momentarily before settling firmly in the northeast direction. Etched around its edge in flowing script are the words: 'Find the Wellspring. It's our only hope now.'

You look up to call to Tua, but as he turns to leave, you glimpse another figure moving in the shadows behind him. For just a moment, you see Korruk—or what used to be Korruk. His form seems partially transparent, like living shadow, his edges blurring into the darkness around him. He locks eyes with you briefly, his expression unreadable, before he disappears behind a wall of fallen stone - the opposite direction of Tua.

The compass in your hands pulses once, then again, seeming to beckon you toward whatever this 'Wellspring' might be."
```
