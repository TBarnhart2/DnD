## Overview
The party's compass, now pointing to the next Nexus Point, will lead them to a submerged or largely underwater location. This Nexus Point is tied to the **Deepdwellers**, and the session will focus on navigating this environment, encountering Deepdweller remnants or guardians, and eventually stabilizing the Nexus. This location will feel oppressive and technologically advanced in a dark, ancient way.


### Location 1: The Abyssal Chasm Approach

![[Pasted image 20250525122005.png]]

**Why they are there:** The Deepdweller Nexus Point is nestled deep within this chasm, within a submerged city. The compass guides them directly to its entrance.

**What the area looks like:** The land here descends sharply, morphing from the shattered urban ruins of the surface into a jagged, treacherous terrain of dark, volcanic rock. A colossal chasm, seemingly bottomless, cleaves the earth before them. A thick, phosphorescent mist, shimmering with faint, otherworldly light, coils in the chasm's lower reaches, obscuring its true depth. The air grows cold and damp, and a distinct, rhythmic thrumming sound, like a colossal, ancient pump operating far below, vibrates through the very ground. On the chasm's lip, massive, corroded anchor points and broken, barnacle-encrusted winches lie abandoned, hinting at the monumental scale of the structures they once held. Twisted shards of dark, ceramic-like material and rusted, intricate gears lie scattered around, distinct in their robust design.

**The Descent - A Perilous Path:** A narrow, crumbling path, barely wide enough for one person, hugs the sheer chasm wall, spiraling downwards into the mist-choked depths. The path is slick with condensation, and loose scree makes footing precarious.

- **Challenge 1: Navigating the Crumbling Path.** Each character must make a **DC 13 Dexterity (Acrobatics) or Strength (Athletics)** check to safely traverse a particularly treacherous section of the path.
    - **Success:** The character maintains their footing and progresses smoothly.
    - **Failure:** The character slips. They must make a **DC 10 Dexterity saving throw** or fall 20 feet, taking 2d6 bludgeoning damage and landing prone on a lower, slightly wider ledge. From this ledge, they can attempt to climb back up or find another way down, potentially incurring additional checks.

**Lore / Discoveries during Descent:** Halfway down, as the mist thickens, the party passes a series of derelict, rusted platforms jutting from the chasm wall. On one, a **Deepdweller schematic etched into a metal plate** is surprisingly well-preserved, depicting a gargantuan, drill-like vehicle powered by Aetherium, designed to penetrate planetary crusts. The design is brutal and utilitarian, showcasing the Deepdwellers' focus on raw power and excavation. A successful **DC 18 Intelligence (Investigation)** check reveals faint Deepdweller runes indicating it was a "Planetary Seeder" – a tool of conquest, not just resource extraction.

### Location 2: The Sunken Vault Entrance

![[Pasted image 20250525122048.png]]

**Why they are there:** This is the heavily fortified and sealed entrance to the Deepdweller Nexus complex, protecting its core from external threats and the crushing oceanic pressure.

**What the area looks like:** The chasm opens into an immense, echoing cavern, its ceiling lost above in the thick, phosphorescent mist. The air here is heavy, humid, and carries the faint, metallic scent of ozone. In the center of this cavern, partially submerged in a murky, stagnant pool of water, stands a colossal, circular airlock or vault door. It is crafted from a seamless, obsidian-like material, adorned with stark, geometric patterns that seem to ripple with a faint, navy-blue light. These patterns depict stylized, multi-limbed creatures and abstract concepts of immense pressure and crushing darkness. The rhythmic thrumming is now a deep, resonant hum that vibrates through the cavern floor, undeniably emanating from beyond the gate. Around the entrance, the corroded skeletal remains of what were once guard posts or maintenance stations are barely visible above the water line, choked with strange, pale aquatic growths.

**Opening the Vault - A Multi-Stage Challenge:** The vault door is not simply locked; it's a complex Deepdweller pressure-seal mechanism requiring specific calibration.

1. **Locating the Primary Control Panel:** A **DC 12 Wisdom (Perception)** check reveals a partially submerged control panel to the left of the vault, its display flickering weakly through algae and corrosion.
2. **Activating the Panel:** The panel requires a surge of external Aetherium to reactivate. A character must spend their action to channel their Aether into the panel. This requires a **DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion)** check (to force the Aether through the corroded conduits) or a **DC 14 Intelligence (Arcana)** check (to properly align the flow).
    - **Success:** The panel's display brightens, showing a complex holographic diagram of the vault's internal mechanisms.
    - **Failure:** The panel sparks and sputters, causing the character to take 1d4 lightning damage. They can attempt the check again on their next turn.
3. **Calibrating the Pressure Seals (Group Check):** Once activated, the panel displays three pressure gauges, each needing to be brought into equilibrium simultaneously. This is a group effort, requiring three successful **DC 15 Dexterity (Sleight of Hand)** checks (to manipulate the ancient controls) or **DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation)** checks (to decipher the fluctuating readings) from three different characters on the same turn. The characters must maintain concentration on these checks; any damage taken requires a DC 10 Concentration check (or half damage if taken from a single source, as per normal concentration rules).

**Encounter - Voidwalker Ambush:** As the party begins the calibration process, the stagnant water around the vault churns. Two **Voidwalker Abyss Lurkers** emerge from the depths, their forms rippling with dark energy and corrosive matter, their multiple limbs scuttling across the wet stone. They prioritize disrupting the characters attempting to calibrate the seals.

- **Abyss Lurkers (Use Chuul stats, with the following modifications):**
    - **Damage Type:** Replace piercing/slashing with **necrotic** (from corrosive void energy).
    - **Grapple:** If a creature is grappled, it also suffers **1d4 acid damage** at the start of the Lurker's turn (representing corrosive void energy).
    - **Action:** Replace "Paralytic Tentacles" with "Void Discharge": The Lurker emits a burst of dark energy in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** or be blinded until the end of its next turn. This ability recharges on a 5-6.
- **Tactics:** Abyss Lurkers will emerge from the water, attempting to grapple the characters focused on the control panel, pulling them into the water to break their concentration. They will use Void Discharge to disrupt ranged attackers.

**Outcome:** Upon successful calibration and defeating the Lurkers, the colossal vault door hisses, groans, and slowly retracts into the obsidian walls, revealing a dark, water-filled tunnel beyond.

### Location 3: The Deepdweller Arteries (Travel)

![[Pasted image 20250525122248.png]]

**Why they are there:** This network of flooded tunnels and chambers leads to the central Nexus Chamber. The Voidwalkers have established a defensive line here.

**What the area looks like:** Beyond the vault, the air is heavier, tinged with a distinct, metallic tang. They enter a labyrinth of deep, angular corridors and chambers, some entirely submerged, others only partially, with the water reaching chest high. The omnipresent rhythmic thrumming is now a powerful, constant vibration that resonates through their bones. Ancient, corroded pipes snake along the walls and ceilings, leaking small amounts of iridescent, viscous fluid that glows faintly in the gloom. Bioluminescent fungi cling to the walls, casting an eerie, pale light that struggles against the perpetual darkness. The overall feeling is one of oppressive, ancient technology, slowly decaying.

**Lore / Discoveries during traversal:** As the party navigates a particularly wide, flooded corridor where light is scarce, they discover a **flickering holographic projector** embedded in a wall, its power source weak. With a successful **DC 13 Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Perception)** check, they can activate it.

- **Holographic Projection (Visual and Audio):** The projector displays a loop of a distorted, ancient recording. A bulky Deepdweller figure, its body covered in dark, almost bio-mechanical augmentations, stands before a vast, underground Aetherium mine. Its voice, deep and resonant, is barely audible: "_...The surface dwellers squander the Aether, blinded by their 'sun-drenched' ideals. We, the Deepdwellers, understand its true nature. It is power. It is control. We harvest it, we refine it, and with the Shadowborn, we shall reshape this world into one of true order. Their sun will be extinguished, and our dominion of the depths will extend to the very heavens._" The projection then shows glimpses of Deepdweller engineers crafting massive, Aetherium-fueled weaponry, and then fades to static.

**Encounter - Voidwalker Garrison:** Mid-way through the arteries, the party enters a large, partially submerged chamber. Two **Voidwalker Core Guardians** rise from the murky water, blocking their path. These creatures are here to protect the Nexus.

- **Voidwalker Core Guardians (Use Galeb Duhr stats, with the following modifications):**
    - **Type:** Construct, infused with Void energy.
    - **Damage Type:** Their attacks deal **necrotic** damage instead of bludgeoning.
    - **New Action: Pressure Shockwave (Recharge 5-6):** The Core Guardian slams its mass into the ground (or water). Each creature within 15 feet of the Guardian must make a **DC 14 Constitution saving throw** or take 2d6 thunder damage and be knocked prone.
    - **Immunity:** Immune to exhaustion, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned.
- **Tactics:** The Core Guardians will advance slowly but relentlessly, using Pressure Shockwave to knock characters prone and break formation. They will try to box in ranged attackers and focus their powerful necrotic slams on the most immediate threats. Their robust nature makes them a tough, grinding fight.

Any creature starting its turn in or moving into this area takes **1d6 acid damage**. This zone persists for the remainder of the combat.

### Location 4: The Deepdweller Nexus Chamber

![[Pasted image 20250525122306.png]]

**Why they are there:** This is the heart of the Deepdweller complex and the Nexus Point itself, the source of the persistent thrumming and their ultimate objective.

**What the area looks like:** The thrumming reaches a deafening crescendo, vibrating through the very air. The chamber is immense, a vaulted space carved from seamless, black, obsidian-like rock, far grander and more intact than previous areas. At its heart lies a vast, circular pool of swirling, raw, navy-blue Aetherium, glowing with an intense, raw power. This pool is the Nexus Point. Strange, intricate Deepdweller machinery, covered in massive pipes and conduits, extends from the chamber walls like colossal, metallic tentacles, plunging into the Aetherium pool. The air is thick with the sharp tang of ozone and a faint scent of iron. The floor is slick with condensation and runoff from dripping pipes. Several massive, reinforced Deepdweller pressure doors, sealed shut, lead to other, unknown sections of the complex. The atmosphere here is one of contained, immense power.

**Lore / Discoveries within the Nexus:** Near the Aetherium pool, on a partially functional console, a **flickering holographic display** cycles through corrupted data logs. With a **DC 16 Intelligence (Investigation)** check, the party can stabilize the display for a few moments, revealing fragmented images and text.

- **Fragmented Data Log:** The display shows images of Razor, shrouded in his Shadowborn cloak, standing within this very chamber before the cataclysm. Text overlays are distorted, but a few phrases are discernible: "_...Nexus synchronization complete... preparations for Lens activation... Deepdweller conduits primed... Project 'Abyssal Ascent' initiated... all non-Aetherborn to be culled._" The log reinforces that Razor was here, manipulating this Nexus as part of his larger plan, and confirms the catastrophic intent behind his actions.

##### Combat Modifiers

- **Slick Floor:** If a character moves more than half speed, they make a **DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check** or fall prone.
- **Aetheric Surge (Random Event):** Roll 1d6 at the start of **Rounds 3, 6, 9**.
    - **1-2 (Minor Leak):** Random Defender/Stabilizer makes **DC 12 Dexterity save** or takes 1d6 acid damage.
    - **3-4 (Empowering Flicker):** Random Defender/Stabilizer gains 1d4 temporary HP.
    - **5-6 (Disruptive Wave):** All creatures within 10 ft. of Nexus (including Stabilizers) make **DC 13 Constitution save** or are pushed 5 ft. away.

---



- **Level Up:** The 2 characters who contributed the _most_ to the stabilization (having the highest number of successful checks) gain a level up at the end of the session.

#### Stabilizing the Nexus
1 person needs to stabilize the Nexus by making the Arcane checks. Additionally, when in the room, you see 6 buttons scattered around the room that need to be pressed throughout the minute. The button will change every round, and the person needs to spend the whole round (after moving) channeling into the button (this includes concentration, and they will be a target). 

### Aftermath:
Upon successful stabilization of the Deepdweller Nexus, the roaring thrum in the chamber softens to a gentle hum. The swirling Aetherium pool stabilizes, its navy-blue glow becoming warm and steady. A sense of immense, ancient power, now contained and at peace, permeates the chamber. The Voidwalkers, if any remain, are weakened and quickly retreat, their purpose defeated.

The compass in the party's possession shifts its glow once more, now emanating a distinct, bright yellow hue. It spins, then settles, pointing them towards their next destination: a distant, cloud-shrouded peak, the likely location of the **Sunborn Nexus Point**. The party now has a brief moment to rest, recover, and process the dark truths they've uncovered about the Deepdwellers' alliance with the Shadowborn and Razor's sinister plans.
