## Plans
- Have them travel to the temple
- They go through the dungeon
- They beat the big bad
- The lens is missing (When they go back, Tua will have heard of a black market sale of the lens)


#### Room 1: Water Pool
This room has a large water pool in the center with a table in the back. When the table is interacted with, a water elemental will appear out of the water, entering a combat.
![[Pasted image 20250207094057.png]]
*Half the HP, no Multiattack, and no resistance to non-magical attacks*
Once beaten, the sands will shift, unlocking a door to Room 2

#### Room 2: Golden Statue
In this room, there is a large golden pharaoh statue in the northwestern corner of the room. On the bottom, there is a plaque that says 
```
"The key is all that you can see, use what you find to go through me, but be aware of what you use, for that with life shall be renewed."
```
The pharaoh has his arms out with his hands open. Whatever the party puts on each hand comes to life and they have to fight it. They must fill both hands, and then the door will open to the hallway, where Room 3 is, as well as the doors through the main entrance to Room 4.

#### Room 3: The 5 Coffin Room
Each coffin has a different weapon engraved on the top - a greataxe, a dagger, a shortsword, a mace, and a bow. When placed on top of the corresponding coffin, it will absorb the weapon and turn it to stone, rendering it useless.

In room 4, there's a table with weapons in it, including all 5 of these.

When all 5 are placed, the door to room 6 will open.

#### Room 4: The Weapons/Sphinx Room
This room has a table of weapons on the west wall, and a large sphinx sand statue on the east wall.
The weapons on the table are the 5 weapons for Room 4. They also are -1 weapons, as they are frail.

The Sphinx gives 4 riddles
1. What is always coming, but never arrives? (tomorrow, the future)
2. I have cities, but no houses; forests, but no trees; and water, but no fish. What am I? (A map/globe)
3. What has no voice but can still tell stories? (A book, a painting, a scar, a ruin)
4. What faction of Aetherborns first discovered Aetherium? (Stonebinders)
Each incorrect guess is a wis save or 1d6 psychic damage (DC 16)

Once all are answered correctly, the door to Room 5 will open

#### Room 5: The Coffin/Boss TP Room
Inside is a coffin at the end of the room, which will TP players to the boss arena once sat in
There is also a statue in the room that will talk to the players if spoken to, and will answer 3 questions truthfully that the players ask

```
As you enter this room, you see a large stone coffin on a higher level than the one you currently stand on. In the Southeast corner, there stands a stone statue of an elderly woman, holding a book in 1 hand and a cane in the other. You also see a table with various pots and books on it.
```

The table contains nothing useful
The statue will only speak if she is touched or spoken to

#### Final Boss Room
The final boss is a large stone golem that is made of sand

![[Pasted image 20250207132557.png]]

Cut HP to 80 and AC to 15
Remove magic damage immunity
Prof Bonus +2
SLAM - +8 to hit, 2d6+2 Blud Damage


#### Treasure Room
This room is the room with the pyramid, which the top opens up and shows a note with the black thorn symbol on it
The lens piece would be sitting in a small pedestal, but is replaced with the note
