## The Building
The building is a simple layout, with a single lower main area in which the auction takes place. Towards the back of the building there is a large staircase up to a second level, which looks down upon the auction area.

Above the center area is a large chandelier, as well as 2 large skylights above the center areas. The walls have some small windows. There seems to be a door in the back of the building that leads below the left upper area, which is likely a storage room/loading dock

The right 1st floor area is a set of offices as well as a safe, which holds all of the auction items.

#### Tables
###### **Table 1: The Antiquarian Society of Eldoria**

- **Group Description:** This table is occupied  by a group of academics and scholars. They are elderly elves and humans, all with an air of quiet intellectualism. They lean in to whisper amongst themselves, consulting notes on scrolls and tablets, more interested in the historical significance than the raw power of the items. They seem less concerned with bidding fiercely and more with meticulously documenting everything.
- **Named Member:** **Professor Eldrune Meadowlight**
- **Style of Dress:** Professor Meadowlight is an elderly half-elf woman with long, braided grey hair interwoven with silver threads. She wears simple but elegant robes of deep indigo velvet, adorned with subtle embroidery of arcane symbols in silver thread. She wears spectacles perched on her nose and constantly adjusts them as she examines her notes.

###### **Table 2: The Syndicate of Shifting Sands (Table is mostly empty)**

- **Group Description:** This table looks like it _should_ be full, but currently only one person sits there, radiating an aura of impatience and wealth. The table is set for four, with expensive drinks and delicacies untouched. The single occupant keeps glancing towards the entrance, tapping a finger impatiently on the polished tabletop. They seem to be waiting for others to arrive, and their expensive, ostentatious clothing suggests they are accustomed to being kept waiting.
- **Named Member:** **Lord Azimuth Kharja**
- **Style of Dress:** Lord Kharja is a powerfully built human man with tanned skin and sharp, hawk-like features. He is dressed in flamboyant desert-themed finery – flowing robes of crimson silk trimmed with gold, numerous heavy gold rings on his fingers, and a large turban adorned with a single, dramatically placed peacock feather. He carries a jeweled scimitar at his hip, though it seems more for show than function at this event.

###### **Table 3: The Ironclad Brotherhood**

- **Group Description:** This table is occupied by a group of heavily armored individuals. Dwarves and half-orcs mostly, they are clad in functional, if somewhat battered, plate armor, their faces grim and stoic. They are largely silent, observing the auction with intense, almost predatory gazes. They seem less interested in history or arcane lore and more focused on the practical applications of powerful items for martial purposes.
- **Named Member:** **Grimbeard Stonefist**
- **Style of Dress:** Grimbeard Stonefist is a dwarf with a long, braided beard interwoven with iron rings and studs. He wears full plate armor, polished to a dull sheen, practical and functional. His helmet rests on the table beside him, revealing a scarred face and a single, piercing blue eye that constantly scans the room. He carries a massive warhammer resting across his lap, though it's obviously not permitted to be wielded in the auction hall.

###### **Table 4: The Whispering Coven**

- **Group Description:** This group is noticeably more unsettling than the others. They are a mix of humans and tieflings, draped in dark, flowing robes and adorned with strange jewelry made of bone and obsidian. They speak in hushed whispers, their gazes often darting around the room as if expecting unseen observers. There is a distinct air of arcane energy and perhaps something darker emanating from their table, making other guests subtly give them a wider berth.
- **Named Member:** **Seraphina Shadowbrook**
- **Style of Dress:** Seraphina Shadowbrook is a tiefling woman with long, black, braided hair interwoven with strands of silver chain. She wears deep purple velvet robes that are almost black in the dim light, heavily adorned with silver jewelry featuring stylized skulls and crescent moons. Her crimson eyes seem to gleam unnaturally in the candlelight, and she constantly fiddles with a string of prayer beads made of polished black bone.

###### **Table 5: The Black Thorn**

- This group is wearing general adventuring gear, and they look pretty gnarly. They place bids when the prices are low, but they never bid on anything when numbers get high. They are the people who help drive the prices up and will win the auction if it doesn't get to a high enough value. 
- They all have the black thorn tattoo on their left pinky finger

## The Dream

```
The white room shatters, not gently, but violently. Searing heat blasts outwards, replacing the white with a roaring orange glow. You stumble, suddenly finding yourself on harsh, uneven ground - black, cracked earth hot even through your boots. Smoke stings your eyes, and the air is thick with the metallic tang of blood, and a sharp, acrid smell – ozone and burning. Chaos erupts around you. Figures clash in a brutal dance of death.

To your right, graceful, unsettling **Moonwhisperers** in shimmering silver robes weave spells of illusion and shadow. It pulses with inner light, a vortex of white-hot energy swirling at its heart. One Sunborn raises their hands, chanting in a language that vibrates in your bones. The vortex flares, then _erupts_.

The dream is gone, but the heat, the deafening roar, and the horrifying image of that expanding white annihilation on the horizon… they linger, chillingly real. The polished wood and tapestries of the auction suddenly feel thin, fragile, against the memory of such raw, destructive power you just witnessed unleashed.

## The Auction
The auction takes place at a small museum in the upper part of Dracordia. The auction lasts 2 days, and the lens piece comes the second day

```
A tall and thin Elven man walks up to the front podium, and you hear the room gradually go silent. 
"Welcome all to the Auction. As you all are aware, each item will be brought up to the stage and shown off. After introducing the item, the bidding will begin. The beginning price of all items will be 100GP, and will go up in 25GP increments. All of you have received a bid card with your invitation, which you will use to bid. 

After 10 seconds of no bid increases, the auction will close, and a 15 min break will insue as we confirm the bid with it's new owner and prepare the next item. All items will be given to the winning bid when the auction ceases. We will begin in a moment. Thank you."

The man steps off the stage and walks through a door to the right.

#### Auction Items
###### First Day
**1. The Aeliana Blade (Weapon - Longsword)**

- **Historical Meaning:** This elegant longsword is said to have belonged to **Aeliana the Swift**, a legendary Elven general who led the unified Elven armies to victory during the Great Sundering Wars, centuries ago. Aeliana was renowned for her speed, tactical brilliance, and unwavering resolve. It is believed the blade was forged by master Elven smiths and blessed by Sylvan spirits before each major battle. After Aeliana's death, the blade vanished, becoming a mythical artifact whispered about in Elven history and sought after by collectors. Its reappearance would be a major event, especially among Elves.
- **Effect on Wielder:**
    - **Swift Strikes:** The blade feels exceptionally light and balanced in the hand. The wielder gains a +1 bonus to attack rolls and initiative rolls when wielding it.
    - **Echoes of Command:** Once per day, as a bonus action, the wielder can evoke Aeliana's spirit of command. For the next minute, allies within 30 feet who can hear the wielder gain advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. _This effect reflects Aeliana's legendary leadership and inspires those around the wielder._
- **Auction Appeal:** High to very high. Elven nobles, military strategists, historians, and collectors of Elven artifacts would bid fiercely. The historical significance and moderate combat/support boosts make it desirable. ***850 GP***

**2. The Whispering Amethyst (Jewelry - Amulet)**

- **Historical Meaning:** This large, deep purple amethyst is encased in a delicate silver setting and hangs from a fine chain. It is said to have been crafted for the **Oracle of Eldoria**, a mysterious figure from a forgotten, pre-human civilization. The Oracle was said to commune with the very fabric of reality, receiving visions of the past, present, and future through the amethyst. The Oracle's prophecies guided her people for generations until their civilization mysteriously vanished. The amethyst is rumored to still hold echoes of those prophecies, though deciphering them is a perilous endeavor.
- **Effect on Wielder:**
    - **Whispers of the Past:** While wearing the amulet, the wielder occasionally hears faint whispers, sometimes unintelligible, sometimes fleetingly clear. These whispers are echoes of past events, mostly inconsequential, but sometimes they offer glimpses of forgotten lore, locations, or even dangers. _This is largely flavor and roleplaying, but could provide clues or hints._
    - **Occasional Glimpse (Risky):** Once per week, the wearer can attempt to focus on the amethyst and delve into its prophetic echoes. The player makes a Wisdom (Insight) check (DC 15). On a success, they receive a cryptic vision about a future event, or a clearer fragment of past lore (DM's discretion). On a failure, they are overwhelmed by the fragmented whispers and suffer psychic feedback, taking 1d4 psychic damage and having disadvantage on Wisdom checks for the next hour. _This represents the unpredictable and sometimes dangerous nature of prophetic magic._
- **Auction Appeal:** Medium to High. Scholars of ancient civilizations, diviners, and those seeking hidden knowledge would be interested. The risky prophetic element and historical mystery add intrigue.  ***500 GP***

**3. The Astrolabe of the Star-Seers (Tool - Astrolabe)**

- **Historical Meaning:** This intricately crafted astrolabe is made of polished bronze and inlaid with celestial charts and symbols using mithril and electrum. It is attributed to the **Order of the Star-Seers**, a reclusive monastic order that flourished centuries ago, dedicated to charting the heavens and divining magical energies through astrological alignments. The Order was rumored to possess incredible magical power tied to celestial events, but they disappeared after a catastrophic meteor shower, their knowledge and artifacts largely lost. This astrolabe is one of the few remaining relics, said to be attuned to potent celestial energies.
- **Effect on Wielder:**
    - **Celestial Guidance:** When used as a focus for divination magic, the wielder gains a +1 bonus to spell save DC and spell attack rolls for divination spells.
    - **Lunar Empowerment:** Once per day, during a full moon or new moon, the wielder can spend 10 minutes focusing on the astrolabe under the night sky. At the end of this period, they can choose to either regain one expended spell slot of 3rd level or lower (full moon) or gain advantage on their next Intelligence or Wisdom saving throw (new moon). _This reflects the Order's connection to celestial power, with different lunar phases offering different benefits._
- **Auction Appeal:** Medium to High. Mages, particularly those specializing in divination or celestial magic, as well as collectors of magical tools and historical artifacts of lost orders, would be interested. The spellcasting bonus and lunar abilities are practically useful. ***550 GP***

**4. The Obsidian Grimoire of Malkor (Book - Grimoire)**

- **Historical Meaning:** This heavy tome is bound in thick obsidian plates and clasped with blackened iron. Its pages are made of a strange, dark parchment that seems to absorb light. It is said to be the personal grimoire of **Malkor the Shadowbinder**, a notorious Archmage from a dark era in history. Malkor was known for his mastery of shadow magic, necromancy, and forbidden rituals. He was eventually defeated and his grimoire was thought destroyed, its dark knowledge considered too dangerous to exist. Its unexpected reappearance sends shivers down the spines of those who know its legend.
- **Effect on Wielder:**
    - **Shadow Whispers (Passive & Dangerous):** Merely possessing the grimoire grants the wielder a sinister aura. Creatures might be uneasy or hostile towards them. Additionally, the grimoire subtly whispers dark secrets and tempting promises in the wielder's mind, requiring occasional Wisdom saving throws (DC 12) to resist its insidious influence. _This emphasizes the dangerous nature of dark magic._
    - **Forbidden Lore (Active & Powerful):** The grimoire contains a wealth of dark magic, including several powerful spells related to shadow, necromancy, and illusion. The wielder can learn spells from the grimoire, but each time they do, they must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 15). On a failure, they gain a temporary flaw related to dark magic's corrupting influence (e.g., increased paranoia, cruelty, fascination with death - DM's choice). _This offers power but at a moral and psychological cost._
- **Auction Appeal:** Medium to High, but with caveats. Dark magic practitioners, ambitious mages seeking forbidden knowledge, and perhaps morally ambiguous collectors might be interested. However, many would fear or condemn it, making its auction controversial and potentially attracting unwanted attention (e.g., paladins, concerned authorities). ***1000 GP*** (Heimerdinger)

**5. The Everburning Hearthstone of the Nomad Kings (Relic - Hearthstone)**

- **Historical Meaning:** This smooth, warm stone radiates a gentle heat and flickers with a perpetual, smokeless flame. It is believed to be one of the **Hearthstones of the Nomad Kings**, powerful artifacts used by ancient nomadic tribes who roamed vast, harsh steppes centuries before organized kingdoms arose. Each tribe possessed a Hearthstone, representing their center, their warmth, and their connection to their ancestral lands. The Hearthstones were said to keep tribes safe and prosperous, even in the harshest conditions. When the nomadic ways faded and tribes settled, the Hearthstones were scattered and largely forgotten, becoming symbols of a lost era of freedom and resilience.
- **Effect on Wielder:**
    - **Comfort and Protection:** While within 30 feet of the Hearthstone, the wielder and their allies are always considered to be in a comfortable temperature, immune to extremes of hot and cold. They also gain advantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by environmental conditions.
    - **Beacon of Home (Symbolic & Utility):** Once per day, the wielder can perform a short ritual with the Hearthstone (10 minutes). Afterwards, for the next 24 hours, they always know the direction and approximate distance to the Hearthstone, as long as they are on the same plane of existence. This could be useful for navigation and returning to a base camp. _This emphasizes the Hearthstone's connection to home and safety for wanderers._
- **Auction Appeal:** Medium. Explorers, travelers, adventurers who value survival and resilience, and those interested in nomadic cultures would find it appealing. The practical utility of environmental protection and the symbolic value of "home" make it desirable, though less overtly powerful than some other items. ***650 GP***
