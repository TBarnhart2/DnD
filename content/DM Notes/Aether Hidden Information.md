## Aetherborn Groups
#### The Whisperwind

- **Location:** Hidden villages scattered throughout remote, untamed regions (deep forests, secluded mountain valleys).
- **Known:** Largely unknown to the wider world. Some are rumored to be mystical beings or spirits of nature.
- **Goals:** To preserve their connection to the raw, untamed Aether and protect the natural world from the potential excesses of Aetheric technology.
- **Historical Significance**: Their deep connection to nature and the untamed Aether has likely influenced the development of many early religions and spiritual practices. Their reverence for the natural world may have played a role in the preservation of ancient forests and the development of sustainable agricultural practices in certain regions.


#### The Sunborn

- **Historical Significance**: Though largely hidden, their understanding of solar energy may have indirectly influenced architectural styles, agricultural practices, and even the development of early calendars and timekeeping systems. Their legends may have inspired myths and stories about sun gods and solar deities.

#### The Moonwhisperers

- **Historical Significance**: Their connection to the lunar cycles likely influenced early navigation, fishing practices, and agricultural planning. Their legends may have inspired stories of moon goddesses, werewolves, and other creatures of the night.


#### The Stonebinders

- **Historical Significance**: Their connection to the lunar cycles likely influenced early navigation, fishing practices, and agricultural planning. Their legends may have inspired stories of moon goddesses, werewolves, and other creatures of the night.


#### The Shadowborn AKA The Damned

- **Historical Significance**: The Shadowborn, with their focus on secrecy and subterfuge, may have played a significant role in shaping the political and social landscape. Their influence may be seen in the development of secret societies, espionage networks, and even the concept of individual liberty.


#### The Skyward

- **Location**: Sky islands, floating cities, and hidden aerial communities.
- **Known**: Legends and myths of flying people, celestial beings, or those who have ascended beyond the mortal plane.
- **Goals**: To preserve their unique way of life and protect the delicate balance of the skies. They may also seek to understand the nature of Aether and its relationship to the celestial bodies.
- **Historical Significance**: The Skyward may have been responsible for the development of early forms of aerial navigation and communication, their knowledge lost to time and forgotten legends.


#### The Deepdwellers

- **Location**: Subterranean cities, underwater caverns, and hidden realms beneath the earth's surface.
- **Known**: Folklore and myths of creatures of the deep, beings of the underworld, or those who dwell in the shadows.
- **Goals**: To protect the secrets of the earth's core and the delicate balance of the subterranean world. They may also seek to understand the origins of Aether and its connection to the earth's energy.
- **Historical Significance**: The Deepdwellers may have been responsible for the development of early mining techniques, the understanding of geological formations, and the creation of subterranean structures.

## The Aetheric Lens
![[Pasted image 20250124174742.png]]
A device that allows the user to see the flow of Aether, not just in themselves or in objects, but in the very fabric of reality. This would be incredibly valuable for:

- **Aetheric Engineers:** To optimize their designs, identify weaknesses in their creations, and even predict potential malfunctions.
- **Explorers:** To navigate treacherous terrain, locate hidden Aetheric nodes, and even find lost civilizations or hidden treasures.
- **Aetherborn:** To understand their own abilities better, control their powers more precisely, and even communicate with other Aetherborn across vast distances.

When a party member attunes to the Aetheric Lens, they gain the ability to cast the _Detect Magic_ spell once per long rest. Additionally, while attuned, the party gains advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks to notice hidden objects, creatures, or traps concealed by magical means.
