## **Session 1: The Symbol and the Fall**

**Introduction (Setting the Scene)** The session begins on a bustling thoroughfare in Dragon's Eye, the capital of Dracordia. Describe the awe-inspiring sights of the city: towering spires connected by elegant sky-bridges, magnificent airships gliding between them, and the hum of Aetherium technology that powers everything from streetlamps to automated carts. Also, describe the stark reality on the ground: the clear and present class divide, with finely dressed government officials and merchants ignoring the impoverished laborers and beggars who live in the shadows of the city's progress.

---

### **Part 1: The Job (The Hook) (Approx. 45 minutes)**

- **Scene:** The four player characters, strangers to one another, are going about their day. Each is approached by a fast-moving street urchin who presses a sealed envelope into their hand and disappears into the crowd before they can be questioned.
    
- **The Note:** Inside each envelope is a small sum of gold and a note written in clean, simple script. The notes read:
    
    > "A delivery is required. The attached item must be placed at the base of the Founder's Statue in the Grand Plaza by midday. Upon completion, a larger payment will be wired to an account in your name. Do not be late. Do not ask questions."
    
- At the bottom of each note is an identical, strange symbol: a stylized, intricate knot.
    
- **The Items:** With each note is a different, seemingly inert object:
    
    - A heavy, polished copper coil.
        
    - A flawless, fist-sized crystal lens.
        
    - A small, lead-lined box that is cool to the touch.
        
    - A brass clockwork mechanism that is not ticking.

##### Elyse
**The Interruption:** As Elyse navigates the crowd, a voice, sharp with urgency, cuts through the noise, calling his name. He turns to see a bronze-scaled Dragonborn woman pushing through the throng towards him. He recognizes her instantly, despite the years: **Verra**, a woman he had a brief but intense relationship with about four years ago. She looks far different from how he remembers; her scales have lost their vibrant sheen, her eyes are wide with panic, and she carries the weary look of someone who has been running for a long time.

**The Revelation:** Verra grabs his arm, her grip surprisingly strong, and pulls him into a narrow, secluded alley between two merchant stalls. There is no time for a gentle reunion. "Elyse, thank the gods I found you," she says, her voice a low, ragged whisper. "There's no time. I'm in deep trouble. I have to get out of Dragon's Eye. Right now."

She then turns and pushes a small child forward from where she was hiding behind her cloak. The girl is about four years old, with her father's elven ears but with flecks of bronze and gold shimmering in her wide, frightened eyes. A small patch of fine, iridescent scales is visible on her neck, just below her hairline. She clutches a worn-out, stuffed dragon doll.

"This is **Kaida**," Verra says, her voice breaking. "She's yours, Elyse. Your daughter."

**The Plea and Departure:** Before Elyse can fully process the shock, Verra presses a small, hastily packed satchel into his free hand. It contains a change of clothes for Kaida, a half-eaten apple, and a small pouch with a handful of coins.

"I fell in with a bad crew from the Undercity," Verra explains, speaking quickly as she scans the entrance to the alley. "The kind you can't just walk away from. They know about her. If I don't disappear, they'll use her to get to me. I can't take her where I'm going. It's not safe."

A sharp whistle sounds from the main street. Verra flinches, her eyes filling with terror.

"I'm out of time," she chokes out. She kneels, kisses Kaida on the forehead, and whispers, "Be good. I love you." She gives Elyse one last, desperate, pleading look. "Keep her safe. Please. I promise I'll come back when I can."

Without waiting for a response, Verra turns and bolts, disappearing into the panicked rush of the crowded market.

**The New Reality:** Elyse is left standing in the quiet of the alley. In one hand, he holds the strange, inert device for his mysterious delivery. In the other, he now holds the small, trembling hand of a daughter he never knew he had. Kaida looks up at him, her expression a mixture of fear and confusion. He still has a job to do, and the note was clear: "Do not be late."

##### Braidyn
**Celestine's "Task"** Celestine is approached not by an urchin, but by a figure disguised as a devout priest of a local sun deity. This "priest" explains that he has been guided by visions to seek her out, recognizing her as a fellow warrior of the light.

- **The Note:** The note he gives her is written on fine parchment with gilded lettering. It does not read like a job offer, but like a holy decree:
    
    > "Champion of the Light, the heart of this city, Dragon's Eye, is threatened by a rising heresy from the shadows below. To protect the innocent and sanctify this ground, you have been chosen to place a holy relic at the Founder's Statue, the city's symbolic core. This act of faith will create a ward against the coming darkness. Let your Emperor's light shine through this righteous deed."
    
- **The Symbol:** The strange symbol at the bottom of the note is described to her as a "Sigil of Sanctity," a holy sign of protection.
    
- **The Item:** Her component for the bomb is the polished crystal lens. The "priest" presents it to her in a velvet-lined box, calling it the **"Shard of Consecration."** Through a simple cantrip, he has made the crystal give off a faint, warm glow, making it appear blessed and holy to a casual inspection.
    
Her task is not a job; it is a holy mission to protect the city. She is being manipulated into believing she is preventing a disaster, not causing one.

###### **Unique Roleplay Encounter (Before the Bomb)**

**Scene:** As Saint Celestine walks with righteous purpose toward the Grand Plaza, carrying the "shard," she passes through a bustling market square. Here, she witnesses a scene designed to challenge her worldview and allow for a significant roleplay choice.

**The Confrontation:** A brutish city guard is harassing a goblin street vendor named **Grix**. Grix sells small, intricate clockwork animals made from scavenged parts. The guard, sneering, has kicked over Grix's display stand.

"Filthy rat," the guard snarls, "Your permit is expired. Now, you can either pay the 'renewal fee' directly to me, or I can haul you to the dungeons for vagrancy." The guard is clearly a corrupt official of the World Government, using his power to extort the weak. Grix, terrified, is on his hands and knees, trying to gather his scattered creations.

**The Choice:** This presents Saint Celestine with a character-defining choice before the campaign's chaos begins
    
No matter her choice, this encounter will draw attention to her just moments before the explosion, solidifying her as a memorable figure in the crowd and a prime suspect in the eyes of Captain Thorne.

### **Part 2: The Plaza (The Stakes & The Spark) (Approx. 45 minutes)**

- **Scene:** The Grand Plaza, the heart of the World Government's power. The area is crowded with citizens, officials, and guards.

- **The Convergence:** The four characters arrive at the statue around the same time, drawn by their separate missions. They may notice each other placing similar strange objects at the base of the large monument, however it is not uncommon for those to place "offerings" to the monument
	- **Founder's Monument** - 3 large, bronze figures, with glowing Aetherium "veins" across all of their bodies. There is a female General, a male Legislator, and a female Engineer
    
- **The Explosion:** Just as the final character places their item, the four components activate in proximity. A low, harmonic hum builds for a terrifying second before a devastating, focused blast of white Aetheric energy erupts from the statue. A high-ranking official, Minister Valerius, who was inspecting the monument with his entourage, is instantly killed. The shockwave throws the party to the ground as the plaza is consumed by fire and panic.

### **Part 3: The Chase & The Respite (The Aftermath) (Approx. 1 hour)**

- **Scene:** The smoking, chaotic plaza, which quickly transitions into the labyrinthine alleys of Dragon's Eye.
    
- **The Chase:** Captain Marcus Thorne and the city guard move in, immediately identifying the four characters who were at the epicenter as the culprits. The chase is a multi-stage group skill challenge. The party must achieve **5 successes before 3 failures**.
    
    - **Stage 1: The Plaza (DC 13):** Use the stampeding crowd for cover (**Stealth**), topple a heavy brazier to block guards (**Athletics**), or point in the wrong direction and yell "They went that way!" (**Deception**).
        
    - **Stage 2: The Market (DC 14):** Scramble over merchant stalls (**Acrobatics**), blend in with a group of cowering workers (**Stealth**), or create a major diversion.
        
    - **Stage 3: The Alleys (DC 15):** Pry open a heavy sewer grate to escape underground (**Athletics**) or scale a wall to the rooftops (**Acrobatics**).
        
- **The Hiding Spot:** The party finally escapes, finding refuge in the hollowed-out shell of a collapsed tenement building.
    
**The Scene:** The party collapses against the rubble, breathing heavily. They are bruised, exhausted, and the adrenaline from the escape is beginning to fade, leaving only fear and uncertainty. Elyse is focused on comforting his newfound daughter, Kaida, who is trembling but trying to be brave.

Seeing her father and his new companions so hurt and scared, Kaida's expression changes from fear to one of intense concentration. She looks at the exhausted party members, her brow furrowed with childish concern.

**The Spell:** Kaida steps into the center of the small, sheltered area. She reaches out her small hands, and a soft, warm light—the color of bronze dragon scales—begins to emanate from her palms. A low, gentle humming sound fills the air, like a lullaby without words.

She looks at the four of them and whispers, "Everyone... sleepy now. Be safe."

A wave of irresistible, magical lethargy washes over the party. It is not threatening, but profoundly calming. Before they can question the source, their eyelids grow heavy, the sounds of the city fade, and they all fall into a deep, peaceful, and dreamless sleep.

**The Revelation:** Exactly ten minutes later, they all awaken simultaneously, feeling miraculously refreshed and revitalized, as if they had taken a proper rest. The immediate aches and pains from their escape have faded. **(The party benefits from the effects of a Short Rest).**

They look over to see Kaida, the bronze light gone, now curled up on a pile of old sacks, fast asleep. The exertion has clearly drained her.

The party must now grapple with a stunning revelation. Elyse's four-year-old daughter is a powerful, innate spellcaster. This is an ability far beyond that of a normal child, hinting that she may have the blood of the Aetherborn, individuals born with the innate ability to command the raw power of Aether. Verra's desperate plea for him to protect Kaida now carries a terrifying new weight. She wasn't just hiding her daughter from a street gang; she was hiding a magical prodigy from a world that would seek to control or exploit her.

### **Part 4: The Symbol & The Gatekeeper (The Lead) (Approx. 45 minutes)**

- **Scene:** The ruined tenement, and then "The Rusty Flagon," a grimy, dimly lit tavern.
    
- **The Search:** The party compares their notes and realizes the symbol is their only clue. They must succeed on a **DC 13 group Investigation check** to scour the local district, looking for a match on doors, signs, or graffiti. Success leads them to the tavern.

While searching for the tavern, they will see their faces on numerous screens around the city, saying WANTED

- **The Gatekeeper:** A faded copy of the symbol is carved into the doorframe of The Rusty Flagon. Inside, the owner is a weary, suspicious old woman named Margot. She eyes them as they enter. This is a critical social encounter.
    
    - **Success (DC 14 Persuasion):** If they approach her politely, explaining they were "sent" and showing the symbol, she studies them for a long moment before nodding toward a back room. "He's waiting."
        
    - **Failure (Intimidation or failed check):** If they are aggressive or threatening, she scowls. "That symbol's a warning. Means 'plague-carriers not welcome.' Get out before I call the guard." This forces the party to find another way into the back room.

### **Part 5: The Kenku & The Path Down (The Direction) (Approx. 45 minutes)**

- **Scene:** The back room of the tavern. It is a small, cluttered storage room filled with barrels and crates.
- 
![[Pasted image 20250820133302.png]]
    
- **Echo the Kenku:** Perched on a barrel, polishing a coin, is a Kenku (a crow-like humanoid). When the party enters and shows him the symbol on their notes, he stops and tilts his head.
    
- **The Message:** Echo perfectly mimics a new voice, a raspy whisper he has obviously heard before: _"They used you. The symbol belongs to the brokers. Find the Sunken Market. Whisper 'Ironbloom.' They'll know."_
- 
- **The Two Paths:** Echo then offers them two routes to the dangerous Undercity where the Sunken Market is located. He does this through gestures and mimicked sounds.
    
    1. **The Maintenance Tunnels:** Echo mimics the _groan_ of heavy iron and the _drip-drip-drip_ of water, then points at the floor. This path requires a **DC 15 Athletics check** to force open a massive sewer main cover and a **DC 14 Survival check** to navigate the dark, crumbling tunnels without getting lost.
        
    2. **The Smuggler's Lift:** Echo mimics the _clanking_ of a heavy chain and the _chink-chink_ of coins. He hops off the barrel and hands them a rusty gear—a token. He points in the direction of the warehouse district. This path requires them to find a specific, bored-looking dock worker, give him the token, and then pay a 20 gold bribe or succeed on a **DC 15 Deception or Intimidation check** to gain passage on a hidden, rickety cargo lift.
        

**Session End:** The session concludes as the party arrives in the Undercity. Describe the massive, cavernous space, the air thick with moisture and strange smells, the glow of bioluminescent fungi on the walls, and the chaotic, sprawling shantytown of the Sunken Market below them. They have escaped, but they have descended into a new, even more dangerous world.

### **Part 6: The Undercity**
**The Sky Above** There is no sun or moon here. The "sky" is the underbelly of Dragon's Eye: a crisscrossing web of massive stone arches, colossal support pillars, and enormous, brass-plated conduits that pulse with the faint, blue light of the Aetherium flowing through them. A state of perpetual, hazy twilight reigns, pierced by the strange light sources of the city below.

**The City Below** Clinging to the cavern walls and pillars is the Undercity itself, a haphazard and layered shantytown that descends into darkness. It looks less like a city that was built and more like one that grew—a metallic fungus spreading over the foundations of its wealthier parent. Rickety metal walkways, glowing with rust and damp, connect jury-rigged homes made of scrap metal and salvaged parts. Water drips constantly from unseen sources, creating a perpetual mist and the endless sound of dripping water.

**The Light and Air** The cavern is illuminated by a chaotic mix of light. Unstable, salvaged Aetherium lamps flicker erratically, casting long, dancing shadows. Patches of eerie, bioluminescent fungi grow in the damp, casting a sickly green or pale blue glow on the walls. Far below, you can see the orange glare of forges and the hum of unregulated machinery. The air is thick and heavy, tasting of damp stone, ozone from leaking Aetherium conduits, and the metallic tang of smog.

**The Sunken Market** From your vantage point, you can see your destination: the Sunken Market. It is the heart of this district, a sprawling, chaotic bazaar built on the cavern floor. The murmur of a large crowd, the clang of hammers on metal, and the smell of strange cooking spices drift up to meet you. It is a place of shadows, secrets, and survival—a stark and brutal reflection of the inequality that plagues the world of Thaia. You have escaped the grasp of the city guard, but you have descended into a new, and perhaps more dangerous, world.
