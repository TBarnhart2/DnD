## **Session 4: The Architect's Truth**

**Introduction (Setting the Scene)** The session opens in the serene, quiet atmosphere of Sabo's tea shop. The scent of jasmine and chamomile hangs in the air, a stark contrast to the grim decision the party must make. Sabo, the White Lotus agent, waits patiently, allowing them the space to debate their future. Veda's offer of escape and a new life hangs in the balance against Sabo's offer of justice and a restored one. This first part of the session is a focused roleplaying scene where the party must commit to a path.

---

### **Part 1: The Decision (Approx. 30-45 minutes)**

**Goal:** The party must decide which deal to honor or if they will forge their own path.

- **Scene:** Sabo's tea shop. He will not pressure them but will answer their questions about the White Lotus and the Shadowborn truthfully, if cryptically.
    
- **The Debate:** The choice is presented starkly.
    
    - **Path of Escape:** Side with Veda. They get away from Dragon's Eye, but the conspiracy continues, and their names are never cleared. They will live as ghosts with new identities.
        
    - **Path of Justice:** Side with Sabo. They have a chance to clear their names and strike a blow against a hidden evil, but they become entangled in a secret war they know little about.
        
    - **Path of Chaos:** They can reject both offers and leak the information themselves, hoping the truth will exonerate them, though it could plunge the city into open conflict.
        
- **The Outcome:** Once the party makes their decision, Sabo will simply nod. If they side with him or choose to leak the truth, he will offer a final piece of advice: "The truth is a weapon. Be mindful of how you wield it." If they choose to side with Veda, he will wish them well, stating, "May you find the peace you seek, even in the shadows."
    

---

### **Part 2: The Intercept (Approx. 1.5 hours)**

**Goal:** Confront the rogue Rising cell and retrieve the message.

- **Scene:** The derelict Aetherium transit station. It is a massive, skeletal ruin. Rusting platforms overlook a central concourse littered with debris. Shattered crystal conduits drip a viscous, faintly glowing residue. The only light comes from the moon through broken skylights, casting long, distorted shadows.
    
- **The Encounter:** The party executes their plan to ambush the message drop. They find the rogue cell waiting: their leader, a fanatical warrior named Kael, and his three most loyal followers. Kael is a true believer in the revolution, convinced that Valerius's peace talks were a World Government ploy to weaken their movement.
    
- **The Combat:** This is the campaign's primary, planned combat encounter. Kael and his cell fight with disciplined, guerrilla tactics, using the multi-level environment to their advantage. They take cover behind debris and fire from the elevated platforms. The goal is to incapacitate them and get the message. A successful **DC 14 Insight check** during the fight reveals that Kael's followers are more scared than fanatical, and a powerful **Persuasion or Intimidation check (DC 16)** could cause one to hesitate or even surrender.
    
- **The Message:** After the fight, the party finds a heavy, coded ledger on Kael. It contains lists of names, Aetherium shipment details, and schematics for other bombing targets. This is the proof.
    

---

### **Part 3: The Architect (Approx. 45 minutes)**

**Goal:** Reveal the true villain and the terrifying scope of the conspiracy.

- **The Reveal:** As the party inspects the ledger, a slow, condescending clap echoes from the shadows of a high platform. A figure emerges, dressed in immaculate, expensive clothing that is utterly out of place. He radiates an aura of immense power and arrogance. This is the Shadowborn handler, who calls himself "The Architect." He is one of the Aetherborn, born with the innate ability to command the raw power of Aether.
    
- **The Monologue:** This is a pure roleplay and revelation scene. The Architect will not fight them; he considers them beneath his notice. He confirms their worst fears with a smirk:
    
    - "You think this is about the government and some pathetic revolution? This city's squabbles are but a useful distraction."
        
    - "My kind, the Aetherborn, once ruled this world, and we will again. We, the Shadowborn, understand that true power isn't debated; it is taken."
        
    - "You were all wonderful little pawns. The perfect catalysts for the chaos we require. Now, your performance is at an end."
        
- **The Escape:** The Architect raises a hand, and raw, purple energy crackles around him. He is not attacking them but the station itself. Support beams groan and twist, crystal conduits overload and explode, and the ceiling begins to rain down debris. This triggers an escape sequence. The party must succeed on a **DC 14 group Athletics or Acrobatics check** to navigate the collapsing environment and get out before they are buried alive.
    

---

### **Part 4: The Final Choice & Epilogue (Approx. 1 hour)**

**Goal:** The party, holding the proof, makes their final, campaign-defining decision. The rest of the session is the narrative outcome of that choice.

- **Scene:** The party stumbles out of the wreckage into the pre-dawn quiet of the Underspires. They are bruised and battered but alive, and they hold the ledger—the truth. This is their final moment of decision.
    
- **The Choice and Consequence:**
    
    1. **They Choose Veda (Escape):** You narrate their tense journey to a hidden airship dock. Veda takes the ledger, her face a mask of cold fury. "They used us. They will pay." She leads them onto a rumbling cargo hauler. **Epilogue:** Your final scene is the party looking down from the airship's railing at the receding lights of Dragon's Eye, a city they are leaving behind forever, their old lives gone, their future an unwritten page.
        
    2. **They Choose Sabo (Justice):** They return to the tea shop. Sabo takes the ledger and bows his head. "You have honored the light and upheld your end of the bargain. Now, I shall uphold mine." **Epilogue:** A few days later, you describe them in a small tavern, watching a news broadcast. A high-ranking military official has been posthumously implicated in the bombing using "newly discovered evidence," and all other suspects—the party—have been formally exonerated. Your final scene is the party walking freely into the streets of Dragon's Eye, their names cleared, but forever changed by the knowledge of the secret war that is fought in the shadows.
        
    3. **They Choose Chaos (Truth):** They leak the ledger to a trusted (or reckless) journalist. **Epilogue:** You describe the firestorm. The news of a Shadowborn conspiracy, of The Rising's manipulation, and the government's incompetence throws the city into chaos. Riots erupt. The government is destabilized, and The Rising fractures. Your final scene is the party watching the smoke rise over the city, knowing they have exposed the truth, but at the cost of the city's peace.
