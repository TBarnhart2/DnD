## **Session 3: Deals in the Dark**

**Introduction (Setting the Scene)** The session begins in the party's safe house at "The Sump." The crude map to the Precinct 9 substation, provided by the information broker Silas, is spread out on a rickety table. The objective is clear: to get the answers they need, they must commit a crime. The atmosphere is tense as they finalize their plan for the heist. This session is about moving from being hunted to being the hunters, and discovering the conspiracy is far deeper than they imagined.

---

### **Part 1: The Heist (Approx. 1.5 hours)**

**Goal:** Infiltrate the Precinct 9 substation and retrieve Silas's Aetheric Listener from the evidence lockup.

- **Scene:** Precinct 9 is a two-story, grime-stained government building wedged between hulking tenements, a lonely bastion of order in the chaotic Underspires. Faint Aether-light glows from its reinforced windows. The party has planned their approach for the quietest time: the middle of the night shift.
    
- **The Challenge:** This is a structured infiltration, designed to reward cleverness and stealth over combat.
    
    - **1. Gaining Entry:** The building has two guards at the front desk. The party must find another way in. Their options are:
        
        - **The Window:** A second-story restroom window is unlocked. Reaching it requires a **DC 14 Athletics check** to climb the crumbling brick facade.
            
        - **The Side Door:** A locked side entrance used for maintenance. It has a simple Aetheric alarm. A character can disable it with a **DC 14 Tinker's Tools check** or open the lock with a **DC 15 Sleight of Hand check** using thieves' tools.
            
    - **2. The Patrol:** A single, bored guard patrols the main hallway in a predictable loop. The party must succeed on a **DC 13 group Stealth check** to time their movements and slip past him undetected, using alcoves and shadows for cover.
        
    - **3. The Lockup:** The evidence room is at the end of the hall, its door locked. It requires a **DC 15 Sleight of Hand check** to pick. Inside, a portly Quartermaster is asleep at his desk. The room is filled with shelves of tagged items. The party must succeed on a **DC 12 group Stealth check** to locate Silas's device—a strange brass contraption with multiple acoustic horns—and remove it without waking the snoozing guard.
        
- **The Complication:** If the party fails a key check (e.g., the group Stealth check), a floorboard creaks loudly. The Quartermaster snorts and begins to wake up. This initiates a tense, timed challenge: they have one round to either hide (**Stealth DC 14**) or snatch the device and flee before he is fully alert and sounds the main alarm.
    

---

### **Part 2: The Name (Approx. 30 minutes)**

**Goal:** Deliver the device to Silas and get the promised information.

- **Scene:** The party returns to Scrapper's Alley and the hidden door to Silas's den.
    
- **The Reveal:** Silas is ecstatic to see his device. He immediately begins hooking it back into his massive listening rig. True to his word, he provides them with their reward—a critical lead. "The man who benefits most from Valerius's death," Silas whispers, "is Commander Grigor, a military hardliner who viewed the Minister's peace talks as treason. But Grigor's too clean for this. A public bombing is messy. It stinks of The Rising. Specifically, a cell that operates out of the old textile mill in the Rustgate district. They're led by a woman named Veda. She's your next step. But be warned, they shoot government spies first and never ask questions."
    

---

### **Part 3: The First Deal (Approx. 1 hour)**

**Goal:** Follow Silas's lead and make contact with Veda of The Rising.

- **Scene:** The Rustgate textile mill is a hulking, skeletal structure, silent save for the wind whistling through broken windows. A single chimney emits a thin trail of smoke, the only sign of life.
    
- **The Test:** As the party approaches the main entrance, four grim-faced revolutionaries armed with salvaged rifles emerge from the shadows, surrounding them. Their leader, a scarred man named Balthus, holds up a hand. "That's far enough. State your business." This is a social test to gain an audience with Veda.
    
    - A direct, honest approach works best. A successful **DC 13 Insight check** reveals Balthus is pragmatic and values straightforwardness. The party should explain they were framed for the bombing and believe someone connected to The Rising has answers. A successful **DC 14 Persuasion check** convinces him of their sincerity.
        
- Veda's Offer:** Balthus leads them inside to meet Veda, a sharp, focused woman in her thirties. She stands over a table covered in maps of the city. She listens to their story intently. "We know you're innocent," she states flatly. "The attack disrupted one of our own operations. It was unsanctioned." She explains she suspects a radical cell within her movement is being manipulated by a wealthy, unknown patron. She makes her offer: "A message is being dropped tomorrow night at the derelict Aetherium transit station. My sources say it's from this new patron. Intercept it for me. Bring it to me. In return, I'll give you what you need: clean papers, new identities, and passage on a cargo airship leaving this continent for good. A way out."
    

---

### **Part 4: The Second Deal (Approx. 30-45 minutes)**

**Goal:** Introduce the White Lotus and present the party with a campaign-altering dilemma.

- **Scene:** As the party leaves the mill, a light rain begins to fall. As they pass a quiet, warmly lit establishment, an old man, Sabo, beckons them in from the doorway. "You look like you could use some tea." His shop is an oasis of calm, filled with the scent of herbs and spices.
    
- **The Counteroffer:** Sabo serves them tea with steady hands. "Veda is a good woman, fighting for a just cause," he says softly. "But she is a pawn in a much older game, as are you." He reveals he is a member of the White Lotus, an organization dedicated to fighting a secret war. He explains the truth: The Rising is being manipulated by an ancient Aetherborn faction, the Shadowborn, who seek to create chaos to cover their true agenda. The "patron" Veda seeks is, in fact, a Shadowborn handler.
    
- **The Cliffhanger:** Sabo makes his counteroffer. "Veda offers you escape. I offer you justice. That message is definitive proof of the Shadowborn's involvement. Bring it to me. I can use it to strike at them and discretely pass evidence to the World Government that will clear your names completely. So, what will it be? Will you run from this city and let the shadows lengthen, or will you help me fight back and reclaim the lives that were stolen from you?"
    

The session ends with the party in the quiet tea shop, holding the weight of two conflicting deals and the fate of the city in their hands.
