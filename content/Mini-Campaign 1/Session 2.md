## **Session 2: The Chief of the Undercity**

**Introduction (Setting the Scene)** The session opens as the party takes their first steps into the Sunken Market. The scale is immense—a massive cavern where the "sky" is the foundation of the city above. The air is damp, thick with the smells of strange food, ozone, and mildew. Your goal is to find "Ironbloom," but you'll soon find that navigating the Undercity's society is more complex than just asking for a name.

---

### **Part 1: The Sunken Market (Approx. 1 hour)**

**Goal:** The party must learn that their lead is a dead end and earn the trust of the locals to be pointed toward the real leader, Sabo.

- **Scene: The Market Floor.** The market is a chaotic maze of stalls made from scrap metal. The party can interact with several NPCs to gather information:
    
    - **Grix, the Goblin Scavenger:** A twitchy goblin running a stall that sells salvaged "topsider" tech—flickering Aetherium lamps, broken clockwork toys, and other oddities. Asking him about "Ironbloom" makes him laugh nervously. "Ironbloom? That's a ghost story told to scare kids. You want real help, you need to prove you're not topside trouble."
        
    - **Thok, the Orc Enforcer:** A massive, old orc with a quiet, intimidating presence. He acts as the unofficial security for the market. He will approach the party, sizing them up. He's not hostile, but he makes it clear that any trouble will be dealt with personally. This is an excellent opportunity for **Saint Celestine** to have her first non-confrontational interaction with an orc who embodies order.
        
- **The Social Quest:** To get a real lead, the party must prove they aren't a threat. Thok will tell them, "Words are cheap. You want people to talk? Show you're not here to hurt us." At that moment, a commotion starts. A merchant's cart has broken, spilling rare glowing fungi all over the path of a large, shambling scrap golem. The party must act quickly to help the merchant before his goods are destroyed. This requires a few successful skill checks (**Athletics** to stop the cart, **Sleight of Hand** to gather the fungi, or **Deception** to distract the golem's operator).
    
- **The Lead:** If they help, Thok gives them a nod of approval. Grix will then approach them quietly. "Okay, you're not so bad. Forget Ironbloom. If you're in the kind of trouble that gets your face on topside news screens, you need to see the Chief. He runs the big workshop over in the industrial sector. He helps those who need it."
    

---

### **Part 2: Journey Through the Chem-Lows (Approx. 1 hour)**

**Goal:** The party must travel from the market to the industrial sector, surviving the environmental hazards and potential dangers along the way.

- **Scene:** The path to the workshop leads through the "Chem-Lows," a district of the Undercity where alchemical workshops and leaking runoff pipes from Dragon's Eye above have created rivers of sluggish, glowing green chemicals. The air is acrid and stings the lungs. The party must make a **DC 12 Constitution saving throw** or suffer a coughing fit, giving them disadvantage on their next Athletics or Stealth check.
    
- **Kaida Trigger Event:** As the party navigates a rickety catwalk over a vat of bubbling green sludge, a rusted pipe overhead groans and bursts, spraying a torrent of corrosive chemicals directly at Elyse.
    
    - **The Magic:** Before the chemicals can hit him, Kaida screams in fear. A shimmering bubble of bronze light flashes into existence around Elyse—her **"Go Away!" (_Shield_)** ability. The chemicals splash harmlessly against the magical barrier, which then vanishes.
        
    - **The Consequence:** The flash of powerful, unexpected magic does not go unnoticed. A small group of chem-baron thugs who were watching from a nearby alley see the display. Believing the party possesses a powerful magical artifact, they emerge to ambush them. This can be a short, sharp **fight or a chase** through the hazardous Chem-Lows, forcing the party to use the dangerous environment to their advantage.
        

---

### **Part 3: The Workshop (Approx. 1.5 hours)**

**Goal:** The party must navigate the social structure of the Workshop and convince Sabo to help them.

- **Scene:** "The Workshop" is a massive, well-lit cavern that serves as the Undercity's heart. It’s filled with the sounds of hammers and machinery, but also the chatter of a community. Sabo is here, but he is busy.
    
- **The Second-in-Command:** The party is first met by **Grakka**, Sabo's lead mechanic and trusted lieutenant. She is the pragmatic orc from the previous prompt. She is fiercely protective of Sabo and the community. She will stop the party, demanding to know their business. This is the primary roleplay opportunity for **Saint Celestine**, forcing her to negotiate with a powerful, intelligent orc who holds all the authority in this situation. The party must succeed on a **DC 14 group Persuasion or Deception check** to convince Grakka they are worth Sabo's time.
    
- **Waiting and Observing:** Even if they succeed, they must wait. During this time, they see Sabo in his element: he fairly settles a dispute between two miners, helps a goblin child (Nix) fix a toy, and directs the complex repairs of a massive air filtration system. They see firsthand why he is the leader.
    
##### **The Meeting with Sabo**

**The Atmosphere** The meeting takes place in Sabo's private office, a spartan, functional room that doubles as a secret command center. The easygoing "Chief" persona the party saw in the workshop is gone. Here, Sabo is a focused, intelligent leader of the revolution. The air is serious. He gestures for the party to sit around a large table dominated by a massive, hand-drawn map of Dragon's Eye and its understructure. He listens intently as they recount their entire story, his sharp eyes missing nothing.

---
###### **What Sabo Knows**

After the party has finished, Sabo leans back, steepling his fingers. He connects the dots for them, sharing his own intelligence and confirming their suspicions.

- **He Confirms Their Innocence and Identifies the Enemy:** "I believe you," he states without hesitation. "This attack was too public, too brutal. Its only purpose was to create terror and chaos. That's not our way. This was the work of a radical splinter group that calls themselves the 'True Flame.' They are a cancer within The Rising who believe that only through fear can the World Government be brought down."
    
- **He Reveals the True Purpose of the Assassination:** "Minister Valerius was not a random target. He was a moderate. I know for a fact that other leaders within The Rising were trying to open secret negotiations with him. His murder wasn't just an assassination; it was the murder of any hope for a peaceful resolution. It was designed to force both sides into a war."
    
- **He Exposes the Deeper Conspiracy:** "Here is what you need to understand," Sabo says, leaning forward intently. "The True Flame are fanatics, but they are not geniuses. The plan that ensnared you—using four unaffiliated topsiders as unwitting bomb components—is intricate, precise, and clean. They had help. Someone with immense resources and a much darker agenda is pulling their strings, using them as puppets to sow chaos. We don't know who this 'third party' is yet, but they are the real enemy." (This is, of course, the Shadowborn's manipulation of The Rising ).
    
- **He Explains Their Value:** "You are ghosts," he tells the party. "Officially, you are the criminals. No one connects you to me or my people. That means you can move against the True Flame in ways I can't. You are the perfect, deniable asset to find out who is really behind all this."
    
---
###### **What He Tells Them to Do**

Sabo then transitions from intelligence to action, laying out the party's mission.

- **The Objective:** "The True Flame's new patrons are paying them for the assassination. The payment is a shipment of unstable, weaponized Aetherium crystals, set to arrive at the old Canal Lock 7 tomorrow night. These crystals are powerful enough to level an entire district of the Undercity."
    
- **The Mission:** "I need you to lead a mission to intercept that shipment. Your objectives are threefold:
    
    1. **Primary Objective: Seize the Crystals.** At all costs, you cannot let the True Flame keep that Aetherium. Disable them, destroy them, or bring them to me.
        
    2. **Secondary Objective: Capture the Lieutenant.** The shipment is being overseen by their most ruthless lieutenant, a woman named Zara. Capture her if you can. She may know who is funding them.
        
    3. **Tertiary Objective: Find a Clue.** The shipment itself might carry a clue to the identity of this third party—a symbol on the crates, a unique energy signature, a coded ledger. Find any proof that tells us who we are _really_ fighting."
        
- **The Stakes:** "If you succeed, you will cripple the True Flame, save countless lives, and bring us one step closer to exposing the real enemy manipulating this city. If you fail, the fanatics who framed you will have the power to burn this city to the ground. The choice of how you approach this is yours."
    
---

### **Part 4: The Heist Board (The Cliffhanger) (Approx. 30 minutes)**

**Goal:** The party, now allied with Sabo, plans their mission for Session 3, giving them clear agency.

- **Scene:** Sabo takes them to a hidden command center behind his office. A huge map of the canal lock is on the table, along with scouting reports.
    
- **The Choice:** Sabo presents them with tactical options for the mission. "The True Flame are expecting a frontal defense, but they're overconfident. You have options:"
    
    1. **The Direct Approach:** "Create a diversion at the main gate and hit them while they're distracted. Loud, but effective."
        
    2. **The Stealth Approach:** "Grakka knows the layout of the flooded maintenance tunnels beneath the lock. You can sneak in, but one wrong move and you're trapped."
        
    3. **The Technical Approach:** "I can create an Aetheric pulse that will cause a temporary blackout in the entire sector, allowing you to move in under the cover of darkness. It's risky and will attract a lot of attention."
        
- **Session End:** The session ends with the party making a choice, receiving any specialized gear Sabo can provide (smokebombs, climbing gear, etc.), and having a clear, player-driven plan for the start of Session 3.
